# Musicianship

## Glacier Peak High School Music Program

I joined the school concert band my Freshman year of high school as a trumpet player. I already had a few years of music experience through my middle school band and wanted to continue playing. After doing well in that class first semester, the director (Mr. Morris) allowed me to join the school Wind Ensemble second semester without even auditioning. 
<br>

The Wind Ensemble was an extremely phenominal group; the music programs in the Snohomish School district were all very well established and a lot of them fed into Glacier Peak High School. The Wind Ensemble was made up of mostly upper classmen, so I practiced a lot to keep up with them. That first year was a lot of fun, playing with such a professional group pushed me to improve my own skill. Particularly, my friend Liam Presler showed me how good one could get at playing trumpet with the right techniques and experience.
<br>

I would also get private lessons from my father, Lance Ellis, who is a band director at several schools and plays Principal Horn in the [Cascade Symphony Orchestra](https://cascadesymphony.org/). This would be vital in honing my skills.
<br>

The following year, I also joined the Jazz Band. This was where I played my first solo. It was pretty nerve-racking to be the center of attention, but it went quite well. I stopped playing in Jazz Band simply because it was very early in the morning and I had a lengthy drive to campus. I continued to play in the Wind Ensemble, which was very enjoyable.
<br>

My junior year, I had a pretty important solo in our [Spring Concert](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5LQhRLcZsCI) for the song ["Variations on America" by Charles Ives](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b01IyzV5i2I). I put in a ton of hours of practice to make sure that I played it right on concert day. Here is a clip from that concert:

That year, I also won the Directors Award for my commitment to the program.
<br>

My Senior year, I continued to play for the Wind Ensemble. This year, I was selected as one of the trumpet players to play "Taps" at the Veteran's Day Assembly. Though Taps is a super simple song, when the entire school is listening to you play, messing up is easy. Just take it from the guy who played at [JFK's funeral](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qbNfOpTx5CQ) (2:20). Anyways, my performance went well and my friends said I sounded good and in tune.
<br>

## Glacier Peak High School Drama Program

My sophomore year of High School, the school drama program was putting on The Little Mermaid for our spring musical. This year, they decided to have live music during the performance in the form of a pit orchestra. I auditioned for this and got in! We met multiple times a week to practice the music and prepare for the performances. I also spend considerable time outside of rehearsals practicing the music. It was pretty scary playing such exposed and difficult music, when messing up could ruin the experience for actors on stage. However, our first few performances went great!
<br>

Things got interesting on our fourth performance. Our conductor caught the Covid-19 Virus and had to quarantine during our 4th, 5th, and 6th performances. Because of this, our lead trumpet player, Liam Presler, had to step up as conductor. This meant that most of Liam's extremely difficult and high-range parts went to me! If the amount of pressure wasn't enough before, it sure was now. I gave it my best, and though my playing wasn't perfect (as I hadn't practice much of those parts), I played well and everything sounded great. 

## Montana State University Music Program

After coming to Montana State University, I joined the University Band second semester of my first year. Even though this band is made up of mostly non-music majors and is not an audition group, everyone put a lot of effort into their playing. Our director even said that in his five years of directing at MSU, this was the best U-Band he had ever worked with.
<br>

For our second concert, I was given the solo part in ["March Opus 99" by Sergei Prokofiev](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TOlA7K7FtJ8). This was a very exposed part, and I knew that not just my section, but the entire band was couting on me. I practiced multiple times a week until I had the part down to a tee at high BPMs. However, with just one little mistake, everything can change on performance day.
