# College Education

After reviewing several options for university, I decided to attend Montana State University. I chose Montana State University because it is in a beatiful nature area (Bozeman, Montana), the school is well respected for engineering and computer science, and it was a financially responsible option.
<br>

![alt text](assets/college-education/image3.jpg)

Upon my acceptance to Montana State University, I was granted the Achievement Award. The Achievement Award is given to students who went above and beyond in high school taking challenging classes and performing well in those classes. I was also granted a scholarship from the Snohomish School district.
<br>

I initially chose Computer Science as my major. Later I would add Computer Engineering as a minor. I came into Montana State University with over 80 credits from being involved in AP classes and dual credit classes in high school. So according to the school, I started college as a Junior. By my second semester, I was already considered a senior.

## Freshman Year

Living on-campus was nice; Langford Hall is a very well-run dorm on campus, and it's right next to the Rendezvous Dining Pavillion. Rendezvous has been selected as the number one university dining hall in the nation. It has a very wide selection of food and a great faculty. Additionally, it has a large wellness center with a complete gym, a large indoor pool, a large indoor track, a soccer field, a basketball court, a volleyball court, and even saunas. The computer science program was certainly top notch from the moment I arrived. I immediately got to know the director of the school of computing, [John Paxton](https://www.cs.montana.edu/paxton/), very well as he helped me decide to add computer engineering as a minor, and encouraged me in wanting to challenge myself further in college.
<br>

Additionally, the computer science department was in the process of constructing a new $55,000,000 building for computer science and computer arts. I will be taking 2 classes in this new Gianforte Hall during my third semester of college.
<br>

![alt text](assets/college-education/Gianforte-Hall-Snow-render-scaled.jpg)

Finally, I was invited to attend the San Fransisco Tech Road trip with John Paxton and several other Computer Science students during spring break. This was an excellent opportunity to both learn more about the CS industry by touring tech companies, but also have fun with my peers in the amazing weather doing tourist activities like Alcatraz Island and the Golden Gate Bridge. The tech companies we visited were Google, Vercel, SenseMesh, and Loma. We also interviewed employees from Nvidia and Atrium. Here are some photos from the trip:
