# High School Education

I attended [Glacier Peak High School (GP)](https://gphs.sno.wednet.edu/) in Snohomish Washington.

![alt text](assets/highschool-education/GPHS.jpg)

## Freshman Year

Freshman year of high school went well for me; I had great teachers and got As in all of my classes. Glacier Peak High School was a very well run school with a vibrant student community and an experienced faculty. Starting my high school education off with a 4.0 GPA felt great, and I wanted to keep it up!

## Sophomore Year

This year, I decided to challenge myself; I took AP World. For those not familiar, AP (or "Advanced Placement") classes are college-level classes with content and exams provided by [College Board](https://www.collegeboard.org/). AP World was taught by a very fun and knowledgable teacher, Mr. Morgan. Though the class was challenging, I put a lot of work into it and got an A. Additionally, I took Honors English that year. Though English is generally not my strong suit, I studied hard and achieved an A in the class. 
<br>

In General Chemistry (which is also considered to be an advanced class), I ended up spending time teaching other students science concepts because it helped them and reinforced my own knowledge. Because I got As in all of my other classes, I maintained my 4.0 GPA. Additionally, I took the AP World Exam, getting an 4 (AP Exams are out of 5; a 3 or above will get you college credit at most universities)! Finally, I was given the Spanish student of the year award at the high school for doing well in that class. 
<br>

On top of all of this, I joined the school knowledge bowl, where I would study large bodies of information and recall that information quickly in a competitive setting.

## Junior Year

My junior year of high school, I stepped up the difficulty level quite a bit. I took AP Language, AP US History, and AP Physics. Many people believe AP Physics is the hardest AP class. However, I considered AP Physics to be the most fun class I had ever taken; it was so fun to see math be used so practically to study how the world works. This year, my commitments were starting to stack up; I was playing in the Wind Ensemble, leading on the robotics team, taking three AP classes, and leading as a team captain in knowledge bowl. 
<br>

This year, I became more organized by keeping a [Google Keep](https://keep.google.com/u/0/) todo list and a [Google Calendar](https://calendar.google.com/calendar/u/0/r) calendar. Because of my organization and commitment to academic excellence, I got As in all my classes again, maintaining my 4.0 GPA. Additionally, I got a 5 on the AP Physics test and 4s on my AP Language test and AP US History test. Finally, I was given the Precalculus student of the year award.

## Senior Year

This was the most challenging year yet; I decided to take five AP classes. This includes the class that all the seniors warned was a "GPA killer", AP Literature. It was taught by the National Honors Society faculty advisor, Mrs. Browning. I took the class because I am not one to step down in the face of a challenge. As if I wasn't challenging myself enough, I also decided to skip AP Calculus AB and go straight to AP Calculus BC because AP Calculus BC includes all of the same information as AB, but just as a review at the start of the year before moving to more advanced topics. Additionally, I took AP US Government, AP Computer Science, and AP Statistics. On top of all of that, I was more involved in band, knowledge bowl, and robotics than I had ever been. 
<br>

That year was the "final boss" of my high school education; the hardest classes at the school, tons of commitments, and a 4.0 resting in the balance. However, through some tenacity, work ethic, and a little bit of luck, I pulled through. I got As in all of my classes. I also got the AP Calculus BC student of the year award. Finally, I got 5s on the AP Computer Science exam, the AP Calculus BC exam, and the AP Statistics exam, a 4 on the AP Literature exam, and a 3 on the AP US Government exam.
<br>

At graduation, I took the stage as a valedictorian of the GPHS class of 2025. I was also awarded the Washington State Honors award and the Washington State Principal Scholar award for my academic excellence. Because of my performance on College Board exams, I also recieved the AP Scholar with Honor award and the AP Scholar with Distinction award. Additionally, because I took so many AP classes/exams and I enrolled in so many dual credit courses with [Everett Community College](https://www.everettcc.edu/) and [Edmonds College](https://www.edmonds.edu/), I would have a strong start going into college.

## Conclusion

With all of my commitments and high expectations for myself, high school was very challenging. However, through that struggle, I grew in my organization, tenacity, and determination. I am very thankful to all of the teachers, administrators, faculty advisors, and classmates who made high school such an incredible experience.
